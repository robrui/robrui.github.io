Robert Ruidisch

I am a Master’s graduate in Electrical Engineering from Technical University of Munich (TUM), specializing in Automation & Robotics. My academic and professional journey is driven by a passion for applying AI and machine learning to solve complex engineering challenges.

During my studies, I worked on various projects, including autonomous driving at UC San Diego, image recognition, and biosignal processing. My master’s thesis at Siemens focused on developing a Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning method using Transformer models for automated electrical routing.

Prior to that, I spent six years at Infineon Technologies, where I gained extensive experience in high-frequency engineering and hardware verification. I developed FMCW radar signal models, optimized RF circuit designs, and verified automotive microcontroller systems, deepening my understanding of semiconductor technologies.

My expertise spans machine learning, RF engineering, and hardware verification, and I am dedicated to driving innovation through technology.
